About IM Mission

The Mission Behind Triple School

IM is a missionary organization that aims to accomplish its mission by spreading the gospel to all nations following Matthew 24:14. We regard this vocation as the calling of today's generation — appointing each individual as a missionary.

At the center of all our ministries is Triple School — the sincere Christian training system that trains each person as a whole personal, whole life, and all-directional missionary to build the kingdom of God.

Triple School aims for Christians to experience God personally and live their lives as whole directional missionaries by knowing God. Not only does it support understanding the Bible correctly, but it also helps each person stand firm as a Christian and missionary throughout their entire life.

Understanding Triple School

The Foundation:
Gospel School Perspective

Humans consist of a body, spirit, and soul — distinguished yet united as holistic beings. A faithful Christian does not separate body and spirit with dualistic thought, developing them in God's way. Triple School's foundation comes from 2 Timothy 3:15–17: to be made wise unto salvation, to be whole as the man of God, and to accomplish God's work.

The Bible declares that God has created the world. History is not a coincidence nor circulation — the Christian view of history is linear, with the beginning and the end times.

Christianity vs. Other Religions

All other religions run towards salvation. Christianity is different — from salvation starts the life of faith. Every Christian should grow from their salvation, based on redemption.

Gospel School — In Depth

Christian Perspective

Knowledge about God is the foundation and core of Christian development. The gospel must be understood in depth — not merely as the fact that Jesus died 2000 years ago, but as God's entire dispensation of salvation, its purpose, and method.

For the Christian, their worldview must change. Change in perspective of seeing the world and yourself achieves the development of intellect, emotion, and will of one's character.

One who 'knows' what is right or wrong 'loves and pursues' that which is right and 'applies and practices' it. You need to know the history of salvation, its purpose, and God's method. This worldview helps an individual develop whole personally.

Disciple School — In Depth

Christian Values

A person who forms an evangelical worldview decides their attitude, values, and principle. The most significant value for such a person is piety — resembling God, following and resembling Jesus Christ, the very nature and image of God.

Disciples are 'sanctified people who dwell in the world but not of the world' (John 17:16–17). There needs constant effort to resemble Jesus. Godliness must hate what God hates and do what God loves.

Romans 12:2 — "Do not conform any longer to the pattern of this world, but be transformed by the renewing of your mind." That is godly thought. Living a Jesus-loving life — that is godly life.

The life of a disciple certainly appears as outer change. When the gospel transforms our inner side, our outer side also changes — as people whose inner side has changed willingly follow the command of Christ.

Calling School — In Depth

Christian Influence

Salvation is essentially calling. It is a calling which God calls worldly people through His grace — washing them, making them His children, and having them as part of God's kingdom.

The influence is not about being in a high position nor wealth — it is about influencing all directions of the community and society where one belongs. Christians with vocations must have righteous and good influence as parents or children in family, as a boss or employee at work, as a member of society and country.

Ephesians 1:10 — "To bring unity to all things in heaven and on earth under Christ." The Calling School trains Christians to live toward that day, in every sphere of life.

Theological Meaning

Rooted in Reformed Theology

Triple School is in the same context as the entire Bible — the gospel, discipleship, and calling are three great streams of the Bible that Triple School retains. The Bible aims for the great day in Ephesians 1:10.

Gospel School
Biblical Theology
Forms a Christian worldview through the historical revelation of the Bible — the history of salvation, of God's kingdom, and of the covenant. God expressed His heart through 66 books of the Bible.
Disciple School
Systematic Theology
Firm doctrinal truth becomes the values of Christ to encourage godly life. Godliness is about knowing God — practiced by loving God. You long for holiness when you know His holiness.
Calling School
Historical & Practical Theology
Considers how to accomplish the vocation of mission today as a church living between the first and second coming, reflecting on how God led the history of mission through the church.

The Reformist Meaning — 5 Sola

Triple School is seen through the 5 Sola — the core values of the Reformation. The purpose of Triple School is to recover: by grace alone, in Christ alone, the gospel of by faith alone, the godly life of disciples, and the vocation of God's kingdom.

This generation needs reformation. In the place of truth, human will and reason. In the place where God is master, self-realization. Triple School exists to recover the essence of faith for today's generation.
